  • Elementary Linear Algebra
  • Howard Anton
  • A good book for learning the what but not much about the why. Concepts and proofs are introduced in an easy-to-understand manner, but definitions are often said to be important, but fails to provide any background introductions or follow-ups on applications. Without tying definitions together or to applications, it sometimes feels like going from one definition to the next, despite doing so well individually. It would be much better if author could add colour to key concepts. Great text for an instructor-led course but so-so for self-study. Stopped at chapter 6.
