  • Finish What You Start
  • Peter Hollins
  • It takes focus, discipline, action, and persistence to achieve challenging goals. Procrastination is a key enemy, often results from our endless/detailed planning leading to stagnation; we should instead plan a few steps ahead only as we move forward without falling into the trap of "feeling great but not actually achieving anything". Ways to fight procrastination include adding sweeteners (things you like) to tedious tasks (like watching TV while exercising), progressing incrementally/step-by-step, and reminding youself of the ambitions/aspirations. I myself am subject to lack of focus/discipline/action/persistence, excessive planning rather than executing, and constant distractions (loud music, noisy people) by wilfully reading books in Starbucks when my home is comfortably quiet.
