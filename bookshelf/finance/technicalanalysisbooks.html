  • Considering this was written in 70s, the systems (directional index, RSI, trend/anti-trend) should have been relatively original and complete, no sure about the performance. The point I appreciate is the author's thought on market-regime (when the word wasn't yet invented) that no system works in all markets so we must identify the mode before deploying an appropriate system. The systems described in this book mostly have defined entry and stop points and despite some non-sensical definitions are a step in the right direction based on basic price observations.
