  • Option Volatility & Pricing
  • Sheldon Natenberg
  • A thorough and practical discussion of some key elements of options pricing and trading in a non-mathematical manner. It explains in an easy-to-understand style and focuses on intuition, with simple examples to illustrate. The coverage is comprehensive and appropriate with more advanced items discussed in a friendly manner, from Greeks and dynamic hedging to volatility and strategies. At the end it appropriately reminds that "a model will almost certaintly be wrong...but in the long run traders who are willing to put in the effort required to understand how a model works always seem to come out ahead" and "only a trader who fully understands what a model can and cannot do will be able to make the model his servant rather than his master".
