  • How the Fed Moves Markets
  • Evan Schnidman
  • Written by a politics Phd involved in applying NLP to central bank communications, this is a unique book on trying to decode the Fed's speeches into a sentiment index to gauge its dovishness/hawkiness. However, while I believe the author is sharp and knows the inner workings inside-out, he only verbosely and qualitatively describes how the index (Fed index) is constructed without shedding any light on the data sources and methodologies. All in all, a (intentioanlly?) poorly-written book on an interesting and useful topic.

  • Princes of Yen
  • Richard Werner
  • A well-researched book on Japan's historical economic and financial developments, from pre-war to post-war, with a focus on the Bank of Japan and credit creation. The author frames the discussion into Japan's historical, social, institutional/political, and economic contexts. His main point is that the BoJ used its bank loan rationing to control the economy and it intentionally created and pricked the bubble to create a crisis to force structural changes. There is a documentary is made out of it.
