  • Introduction to Econometrics
  • Christoher Dougherty
  • A very gentle and coherent introduction to econometrics, with a logical progression focusing on intuition and understanding of key concepts, without much use of mathematics (nothing beyond differentiation). It focuses on teaching basic concepts as a textbook, not a reference with overwhelming materials and incoherent coverage, and it does an excellent job in that regard. Each chapter provides background discussion which logically leads to definitions and equation derivations (sometimes with easy-to-follow proofs when appropriate), followed by some examples/simulations illustrating concepts and parameter implications. One intended weakness is that it doesn't involve any matrix algebra so discussions beyond two regressors become qualitative. Also, I find its chapters on binary choice (linear probability, logit, probit) and autocorrelation too shallow (inevitable given lack of mathematical treatment) and difficult to follow.
