  • Essential Guide to Semiconductors
  • Jim Turley
  • Despite written in early 2000s, the basics still valid. It does a good job in explaining the basics to laymen without using any technical terms. It sheds light on some commonly used chips (microprocessors, memory, graphics, communication, microcontrollers, ASIC/FPGA), how chips are designed, how chips are manufactured, and some outdated industry data and statistics. It does the job well without being verbose. Good as a first book for non-engineers.
